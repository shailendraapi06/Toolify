What is GST?

Goods and Services Tax (GST) is an indirect tax used in India on the supply of goods and services. It is a comprehensive, multistage, destination-based tax that has replaced many indirect taxes in India such as the excise duty, VAT, and services tax.

How GST is Calculated

To calculate GST, you multiply the base amount by the GST rate. For Exclusive GST, the tax is added on top of the base price. For Inclusive GST, the tax is already part of the total price, and the base price is derived using the formula: Base = Total / (1 + Rate/100).

GST Rates in India

Currently, India uses four main GST slabs: 5%, 12%, 18%, and 28%. Common household items are usually in the lower slabs, while luxury goods and services fall under the 28% bracket. Our calculator supports quick selection for these standard Indian tax rates.
